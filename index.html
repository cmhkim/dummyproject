Day: 4 Why were the missions shut down?

One reason the mission system began to break down is that many of the Native Americans,
who were coerced into living, working, and being converted in to missions, died off
because of disease brought by the Spanish padres. Those Native Americans who were left often
disliked mission life and tried to run away. By the time Mexico broke away from
Spain and took control of California, the missions were already losing their importance.
When Mexico didn't have enough money to pay its soldiers, it sometimes gave them mission lands
instead of wages. Mission land was also given to Mexican citizens in return for favors to the government.
By 1934, the Mexican government ruled that all the California missions be shut down.
